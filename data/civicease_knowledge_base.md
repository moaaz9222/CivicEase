## Federal Program — SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)

- **Official Full Name:** Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)
- **Lead Federal Agency:** U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA), Food and Nutrition Service (FNS)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** 7 CFR Parts 271–285; SNAP Policy Memos at fns.usda.gov
- **Program Type:** Food Assistance / Nutrition Benefit

### SNAP Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Gross Income Limit:** 130% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL)
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,724/month gross
  - Household of 4: ≤ $3,543/month gross
- **Net Income Limit:** 100% FPL after allowable deductions
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,330/month net
  - Household of 4: ≤ $2,750/month net
- **Asset/Resource Limit:** $3,000 for most households; $4,500 if a member is age 60+ or has a disability
- **Categorical Eligibility:** Households receiving TANF or SSI are generally categorically eligible
- **Broad-Based Categorical Eligibility (BBCE):** Many states expand gross income limit to 200% FPL; states may waive asset tests
- **Work Requirements (ABAWD):** Adults 18–52 without dependents must work or participate in job training ≥ 80 hrs/month (subject to state waivers)
- **Benefit Delivery:** Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) card, issued monthly
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap

## Federal Program — WIC (Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children)

- **Official Full Name:** Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC)
- **Lead Federal Agency:** USDA Food and Nutrition Service (FNS)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** 7 CFR Part 246
- **Program Type:** Food Assistance / Nutrition Benefit (Categorical, not universal)

### WIC Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Categorical Eligibility:** Must be a pregnant woman, postpartum woman (up to 6 months), breastfeeding woman (up to 12 months), infant (up to age 1), or child (ages 1–4)
- **Income Limit:** ≤ 185% FPL
  - Household of 1: ≤ $2,461/month gross
  - Household of 4: ≤ $5,088/month gross
- **Nutritional Risk Requirement:** Must be assessed as nutritionally at-risk by a health professional (a broad standard; most applicants qualify)
- **Automatic Income Eligibility:** Households enrolled in SNAP, Medicaid, or TANF are automatically income-eligible
- **Benefit Type:** Vouchers or EBT for specific approved foods (formula, milk, eggs, produce, whole grains, etc.); breastfeeding support; referrals
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic

## Federal Program — NSLP & SBP (National School Lunch Program / School Breakfast Program)

- **Official Full Names:** National School Lunch Program (NSLP) / School Breakfast Program (SBP)
- **Lead Federal Agency:** USDA Food and Nutrition Service (FNS)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** 7 CFR Parts 210 (NSLP) and 220 (SBP); Richard B. Russell National School Lunch Act
- **Program Type:** Child Food Assistance

### NSLP/SBP Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Free Meals Income Limit:** ≤ 130% FPL
  - Household of 4: ≤ $3,543/month gross
- **Reduced-Price Meals Income Limit:** 130%–185% FPL
  - Household of 4: $3,543–$5,088/month gross
- **Direct Certification:** Children enrolled in SNAP, TANF, or Medicaid are directly certified without a household application
- **Universal Free Meals (CEP):** Schools in high-poverty areas may elect the Community Eligibility Provision (CEP), making all students eligible for free meals regardless of income
- **Benefit Type:** Subsidized or free meals served during the school day in participating schools
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.fns.usda.gov/nslp

## Federal Program — TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)

- **Official Full Name:** Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)
- **Lead Federal Agency:** U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS), Administration for Children and Families (ACF)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** 42 U.S.C. §§ 601–619; 45 CFR Parts 260–265
- **Program Type:** Cash Assistance (Block Grant to States)

### TANF Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Federal Structure:** TANF is a federal block grant. States have wide discretion to set income limits, benefit amounts, and program rules. There are NO single federal income thresholds.
- **Core Federal Eligibility Criteria (families must generally meet all):**
  - Have a child under age 18 (or 19 if still in secondary school) living in the home, OR be a pregnant woman
  - Be a U.S. citizen or qualified immigrant
  - Meet the state's specific income and asset tests
- **Typical State Income Range:** Most states set limits between 30%–60% FPL for initial eligibility
- **Federal Time Limit:** 60 cumulative months (5 years) of federally funded TANF cash benefits for adults; states may set shorter limits
- **Work Requirements:** Adults must engage in approved work activities (employment, job search, vocational training) — typically 20–30 hrs/week
- **Benefit Type:** Monthly cash grant (amount set by each state); may also fund childcare, transportation, job training
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ofa/programs/tanf

## Federal Program — SSI (Supplemental Security Income)

- **Official Full Name:** Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- **Lead Federal Agency:** U.S. Social Security Administration (SSA)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** Title XVI of the Social Security Act; 20 CFR Part 416
- **Program Type:** Cash Assistance (Federal Baseline Benefit)

### SSI Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Categorical Eligibility:** Must be age 65 or older, OR blind, OR have a qualifying disability (for adults and children)
- **Income Limit:** Countable income must be below the Federal Benefit Rate (FBR)
  - 2026 FBR (Individual): $967/month
  - 2026 FBR (Couple, both eligible): $1,450/month
- **Asset/Resource Limit:**
  - Individual: $2,000
  - Couple: $3,000
  - Note: Primary home and one vehicle are excluded from resource count
- **Citizenship/Residency:** Must be a U.S. citizen or certain qualified immigrants; must reside in the U.S.
- **State Supplementation:** Many states add a State Supplementary Payment (SSP) on top of the federal SSI amount
- **Benefit Type:** Monthly cash payment; SSI recipients are typically automatically eligible for Medicaid
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.ssa.gov/ssi/

## Federal Program — EITC (Earned Income Tax Credit)

- **Official Full Name:** Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
- **Lead Federal Agency:** U.S. Department of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Service (IRS)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** 26 U.S.C. § 32; IRS Publication 596
- **Program Type:** Refundable Tax Credit / Cash Assistance (Annual)

### EITC Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026 Tax Year)

- **Earned Income Requirement:** Must have earned income from employment or self-employment
- **Investment Income Limit:** Investment income must be ≤ $11,950 for 2026 tax year
- **Maximum Credit Amounts (2026 estimates, indexed to inflation):**
  - No qualifying children: ~$649 (for single filers)
  - 1 qualifying child: ~$4,328
  - 2 qualifying children: ~$7,152
  - 3+ qualifying children: ~$8,046
- **Income Limits (Married Filing Jointly, 2026 estimates):**
  - No children: ≤ ~$27,000 AGI
  - 1 child: ≤ ~$53,500 AGI
  - 2 children: ≤ ~$59,500 AGI
  - 3+ children: ≤ ~$63,500 AGI
- **Age Requirement (no qualifying child):** Worker must be age 25–64
- **Benefit Type:** Refundable federal tax credit claimed on annual tax return (Form 1040)
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/individuals/earned-income-tax-credit

## Federal Program — Medicaid

- **Official Full Name:** Medicaid
- **Lead Federal Agency:** HHS Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** Title XIX of the Social Security Act; 42 CFR Parts 430–456
- **Program Type:** Healthcare Coverage (Federal-State Partnership)

### Medicaid Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **ACA Medicaid Expansion (Adopted States):** Adults ages 19–64 with income ≤ 138% FPL
  - Individual: ≤ $1,831/month (Modified Adjusted Gross Income — MAGI)
  - Family of 4: ≤ $3,795/month
- **Children (mandatory coverage):** ≤ 100% FPL in all states; most states cover up to 133%–200% FPL
- **Pregnant Women (mandatory coverage):** ≤ 133% FPL federally; most states higher (up to 200%+ FPL)
- **Expansion Status:** As of 2026, 41 states + DC have adopted ACA Medicaid expansion; 9 states have not (AL, FL, GA, KS, MS, SC, TN, TX, WI — verify current status)
- **Non-Expansion State Eligibility:** Adults in non-expansion states face very narrow eligibility (often must have dependent children, disability, pregnancy, or be elderly)
- **Categorical Eligibility:** SSI recipients are typically automatically eligible for Medicaid in most states
- **Benefit Type:** Comprehensive health insurance (doctor visits, hospital, prescriptions, mental health, dental/vision for children) with low or no cost to enrollee
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.medicaid.gov
- **Application Gateway:** Healthcare.gov or state Medicaid agency portals

## Federal Program — CHIP (Children's Health Insurance Program)

- **Official Full Name:** Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP)
- **Lead Federal Agency:** HHS Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** Title XXI of the Social Security Act; 42 CFR Part 457
- **Program Type:** Healthcare Coverage for Children

### CHIP Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Target Population:** Uninsured children in families with incomes too high for Medicaid but who cannot afford private coverage
- **Income Range:** Generally 138%–300% FPL (varies significantly by state; some states cover up to 400% FPL)
  - Household of 4 at 200% FPL: ≤ $5,500/month
  - Household of 4 at 300% FPL: ≤ $8,250/month
- **Age:** Children under age 19
- **Premium & Cost-Sharing:** States may charge modest premiums and copays for families above 150% FPL
- **CHIP Perinatal (Some States):** Extends CHIP-funded coverage to pregnant women and unborn children
- **Benefit Type:** Comprehensive pediatric health coverage; often mirrors Medicaid children's benefits
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.medicaid.gov/chip
- **Application Gateway:** Healthcare.gov or state CHIP/Medicaid agency portals

## Federal Program — CCDF (Child Care and Development Fund Subsidies)

- **Official Full Name:** Child Care and Development Fund (CCDF) Child Care Subsidies
- **Lead Federal Agency:** HHS Administration for Children and Families (ACF), Office of Child Care (OCC)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** 45 CFR Parts 98–99; Child Care and Development Block Grant (CCDBG) Act
- **Program Type:** Childcare Assistance (Block Grant to States)

### CCDF Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Federal Structure:** CCDF is a block grant; states set their own income limits, copayment schedules, and eligibility criteria within federal parameters
- **Age:** Children under age 13 (up to 18 if child has a disability)
- **Activity Requirement:** Parent/guardian must be working, in school, or in job training
- **Income Limit (Federal Ceiling):** States must serve families with income ≤ 85% of State Median Income (SMI); most states set limits lower in practice
  - Typical state eligibility range: 150%–250% FPL
- **Priority Populations:** Children in foster care; children receiving protective services; children of teen parents in school
- **Benefit Type:** Subsidy (voucher or certificate) to pay licensed childcare providers; copayment required on sliding-scale basis
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.acf.hhs.gov/occ/ccdf-program

## Federal Program — Head Start / Early Head Start

- **Official Full Name:** Head Start Program / Early Head Start Program
- **Lead Federal Agency:** HHS Administration for Children and Families (ACF), Office of Head Start (OHS)
- **Federal Policy Reference:** 42 U.S.C. §§ 9831–9852c; 45 CFR Parts 1301–1305; Head Start Program Performance Standards
- **Program Type:** Early Childhood Education and Comprehensive Child Development

### Head Start Baseline Eligibility Logic (2026)

- **Head Start (Ages 3–5):** Children ages 3–5 from families with income at or below 100% FPL
- **Early Head Start (Birth–Age 3):** Pregnant women and children from birth to age 3 with income ≤ 100% FPL
- **Mandatory Enrollment Set-Asides:**
  - ≥ 10% of enrollment slots must be reserved for children with disabilities (IEP/IFSP)
  - ≥ 10% of enrollment may be from families above 100% FPL (at grantee discretion)
- **Categorical Priority:** Children in foster care and children experiencing homelessness are eligible regardless of income
- **Benefit Type:** Free, comprehensive early childhood education (part-day or full-day), health screenings, dental, nutritional support, and family services — all at no cost
- **Federal Benefits Portal:** https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ohs

### California — CalFresh (State SNAP Program)

- **Local Program Name:** CalFresh
- **Federal Parent Program:** SNAP
- **Administering Agency:** CDSS / County DSS
- **Policy Manual:** CalFresh State Plan; MPP Title 63 (CalFresh)
- **Gross Income Limit (130% FPL):**
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,724/month
  - Household of 4: ≤ $3,575/month
- **Net Income Limit (100% FPL):**
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,330/month
  - Household of 4: ≤ $2,750/month
- **California Note:** California operates Broad-Based Categorical Eligibility (BBCE), allowing gross income up to 200% FPL for most households (eliminating the asset test for these households)
  - Household of 1 under BBCE: ≤ $2,660/month
  - Household of 4 under BBCE: ≤ $5,500/month
- **Asset Test:** Waived for BBCE-eligible households; $3,000 or $4,500 (elderly/disabled) for categorically eligible households
- **Special California Rules:** CalFresh Expanded (covers some non-citizen immigrants not covered federally); Restaurant Meals Program (RMP) for elderly/homeless/disabled recipients

### California — CalWORKs (State TANF Program)

- **Local Program Name:** California Work Opportunity and Responsibility to Kids (CalWORKs)
- **Federal Parent Program:** TANF
- **Administering Agency:** CDSS / County DSS
- **Policy Manual:** MPP Title 42 (CalWORKs); CalWORKs Handbook
- **Income Limit (Initial Eligibility, varies by family size and county):**
  - Approximately 80%–130% of FPL for initial eligibility (county Minimum Basic Standard of Adequate Care — MBSAC applies)
  - Household of 4 (illustrative): ≤ ~$3,100/month gross at entry
- **Maximum Monthly Grant (2026 estimates, subject to COLA):**
  - Region 1 (High Cost): Family of 3 ~$980/month
  - Region 2 (Standard): Family of 3 ~$930/month
- **Time Limits:** Adults limited to 60 cumulative months of CalWORKs cash aid; child-only grants continue beyond adult time limits
- **Work Requirements:** Adults must participate in Welfare-to-Work (WtW) activities (employment, job search, vocational education)
- **Special Programs:** Housing Support Program (HSP); Homeless Assistance; CalWORKs Mental Health and Substance Abuse (MHSA) services

### California — Medi-Cal (State Medicaid Program)

- **Local Program Name:** Medi-Cal
- **Federal Parent Program:** Medicaid (Title XIX)
- **Administering Agency:** California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS)
- **Policy Manual:** Medi-Cal Policy Manual (MCPM) — https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/provgovpart/Pages/mmcd_pol_prac_manuals.aspx
- **Expansion Status:** California adopted full ACA Medicaid expansion
- **2026 Income Limits:**
  - Adults 19–64 (expansion): ≤ 138% FPL
    - Individual: ≤ $1,836/month MAGI
    - Family of 4: ≤ $3,795/month MAGI
  - Children under 19: ≤ 266% FPL (covered through Medi-Cal and CHIP)
    - Child in family of 4: ≤ $7,315/month
  - Pregnant individuals: ≤ 213% FPL
    - Individual: ≤ $2,832/month
  - Seniors 65+/Disabled (non-MAGI): Income and asset tests apply; contact county for current SSI-linked thresholds
- **California Expansion (2024 onward):** Medi-Cal covers ALL income-eligible California residents regardless of immigration status (including undocumented adults ages 26–49, expanded in 2024)
- **Delivery Model:** Managed Care Plans (Medi-Cal Managed Care) in most counties; Fee-for-Service in some rural areas
- **Application Portals:** Covered California (coveredca.gov) for marketplace plans; BenefitsCal (benefitscal.com) or county for Medi-Cal

### California — CCAP (State Childcare Subsidy Program)

- **Local Program Name:** California Child Care Assistance Program (CCAP) / Alternative Payment (AP) Program
- **Federal Parent Program:** CCDF
- **Administering Agency:** CDSS, Child Care Bureau; delivered through Local Child Care and Development Planning Councils
- **Policy Manual:** MPP Title 47 (Child Care and Development); California Child Care and Development Finance Guide
- **Income Limit:** ≤ 85% of California State Median Income (SMI)
  - 2026 estimate: Family of 4 ≤ ~$9,000/month (85% SMI threshold; verify annually at CDSS)
- **Copayment:** Sliding-scale fee based on income and family size; families below ~50% SMI typically pay $0 copay
- **Priority Populations:** Children experiencing homelessness; children in foster care; children of parents in school/job training
- **Related Program:** California State Preschool Program (CSPP) — subsidized part-day and full-day preschool at 100% FPL (priority) and up to 75% SMI

- **Young Child Tax Credit (YCTC):** $1,117 credit for CalEITC-eligible families with a child under age 6
- **California RAP (Refugee Assistance Program):** Cash and services for qualified refugees in first 8 months of arrival

### Texas — SNAP (Food Stamps / Lone Star Card)

- **Local Program Name:** SNAP (Texas calls it "SNAP"; delivered via Lone Star Card EBT)
- **Federal Parent Program:** SNAP
- **Administering Agency:** Texas HHSC
- **Policy Manual:** Texas Works Advisor Handbook (TWAH) — Benefits Policy Chapter H (SNAP)
- **Gross Income Limit (130% FPL):**
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,724/month
  - Household of 4: ≤ $3,575/month
- **Net Income Limit (100% FPL):**
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,330/month
  - Household of 4: ≤ $2,750/month
- **Asset Test:** $3,000 (most households); $4,500 (households with elderly/disabled member)
- **Texas Note:** Texas does NOT operate BBCE (Broad-Based Categorical Eligibility) — the standard federal income and asset tests strictly apply
- **ABAWD Work Requirement:** Applies in most Texas counties; limited federal waiver coverage; adults 18–52 without dependents must work or participate in job training ≥ 80 hrs/month
- **Non-Expansion State Impact:** Texas has NOT expanded Medicaid; SNAP income screens are particularly important for Texas households

### Texas — TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)

- **Local Program Name:** TANF (Texas refers to it as TANF; formerly known as Texas Works)
- **Federal Parent Program:** TANF
- **Administering Agency:** Texas HHSC
- **Policy Manual:** Texas Works Advisor Handbook (TWAH) — Chapter A (TANF)
- **Income Limit (Net Income, 2026):**
  - Texas uses a very restrictive income standard for TANF
  - Income limit is approximately 17% FPL net (after deductions)
  - Family of 3 income limit: ≤ ~$455/month net
- **Maximum Monthly Grant (2026):**
  - Family of 1 (parent + child): ~$74/month
  - Family of 3: ~$298/month
  - Note: Texas TANF benefit levels are among the lowest in the nation
- **Texas Time Limits:** 60 months (federal limit); Texas imposes a 12-month limit on assistance within any 48-month period for adults who have not met work requirements
- **Work Requirements:** Participation in Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) Choices program (job search, employment, skills training) is mandatory for most adult TANF recipients
- **Asset Test:** $1,000 in liquid resources; $2,500 total resources
- **Related Program:** Texas Works — job readiness, employment services, and workforce training connected to TANF

### Texas — Medicaid (Non-Expansion State)

- **Local Program Name:** Texas Medicaid
- **Federal Parent Program:** Medicaid (Title XIX)
- **Administering Agency:** Texas HHSC; delivered through managed care organizations (MCOs) statewide
- **Policy Manual:** Texas Medicaid Provider Procedures Manual; HHSC Rules in Title 1, Texas Administrative Code (TAC) Parts 15, 16
- **Expansion Status:** Texas has NOT adopted ACA Medicaid expansion as of 2026
- **2026 Income Limits (Very Restrictive for Non-Disabled Adults):**
  - Non-disabled adults without dependent children: Generally NOT eligible for Texas Medicaid regardless of income (no pathway exists absent expansion)
  - Parents/caretakers with dependent children: ≤ 18% FPL
    - Parent in family of 3: ≤ ~$408/month
  - Pregnant individuals: ≤ 200% FPL
    - Individual: ≤ $2,660/month
  - Children under 6: ≤ 133% FPL (Medicaid)
    - Child in family of 4: ≤ $3,658/month
  - Children ages 6–18: ≤ 100% FPL (Medicaid); 100%–200% FPL (CHIP — see below)
  - Seniors 65+/Disabled (SSI-linked): SSI recipients auto-enrolled; income and asset limits per SSI rules
- **Healthcare.gov Note:** Texans who do not qualify for Medicaid but have incomes 100%–400% FPL can access subsidized Marketplace plans at Healthcare.gov

### Texas — CHIP (Children's Health Insurance Program)

- **Local Program Name:** Children's Medicaid / CHIP (Texas)
- **Federal Parent Program:** CHIP (Title XXI)
- **Administering Agency:** Texas HHSC
- **Policy Manual:** CHIP Rules — Title 1 TAC Chapter 370
- **Income Limit:**
  - Children ages 0–18: 200% FPL (Medicaid covers 0–133% FPL; CHIP covers 133%–200% FPL)
  - Family of 4 at 200% FPL: ≤ $5,500/month
- **Premium:** Modest annual premium for families above 150% FPL (max ~$50/year per family)
- **CHIP Perinatal:** Covers unborn children of pregnant women who are not otherwise eligible for Texas Medicaid; income limit ≤ 200% FPL
- **Application Portal:** YourTexasBenefits.com or call 2-1-1 Texas

### Texas — Child Care (Texas Child Care Subsidy Program)

- **Local Program Name:** Texas Child Care Subsidy Program (Child Care Services — CCS)
- **Federal Parent Program:** CCDF
- **Administering Agency:** Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) and Local Workforce Development Boards (28 boards statewide)
- **Policy Manual:** TWC Child Care Services Policy Manual — https://www.twc.texas.gov/partners/child-care-services
- **Income Limit:** ≤ 85% Texas State Median Income (SMI)
  - 2026 estimate: Family of 4 ≤ ~$6,200/month (85% SMI; verify at TWC annually)
- **Priority:** Families receiving TANF; children in protective services; lowest-income families on waitlist
- **Copayment:** Sliding scale; families at very low incomes may have $0 copay
- **Application:** Through local Workforce Solutions office; waitlists common in many regions

- **STAR Medicaid Managed Care:** Medicaid delivered through managed care plans (Aetna Better Health, UnitedHealthcare, Molina, etc.)
- **STAR Kids:** Medicaid program for children with disabilities under age 21
- **Texas Children's Health Insurance Program (TCHIP):** Branded form of CHIP for Texas children
- **Healthy Texas Women:** Family planning services for women 18–44 at ≤ 200% FPL (separate from full Medicaid)
- **2-1-1 Texas:** Statewide health and human services hotline connecting residents to benefits and community resources — https://www.211texas.org

### New York — SNAP (Food Stamps / EBT)

- **Local Program Name:** SNAP (New York calls it SNAP; delivered via EBT card)
- **Federal Parent Program:** SNAP
- **Administering Agency:** OTDA; Local Social Services Districts; NYC HRA for NYC residents
- **Policy Manual:** OTDA Informational Letters (INFs) and Administrative Directives (ADMs); Food Stamp Regulations 18 NYCRR Part 387
- **Gross Income Limit (130% FPL):**
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,724/month
  - Household of 4: ≤ $3,575/month
- **Net Income Limit (100% FPL):**
  - Household of 1: ≤ $1,330/month
  - Household of 4: ≤ $2,750/month
- **New York BBCE:** New York operates Broad-Based Categorical Eligibility, raising the gross income limit to 200% FPL for most households and waiving the asset test
  - Household of 1 under BBCE: ≤ $2,660/month
  - Household of 4 under BBCE: ≤ $5,500/month
- **NYC Note:** NYC HRA administers SNAP for the 5 boroughs; application and renewals via ACCESS HRA app or ACCESS NYC portal

### New York — Family Assistance (FA) — State TANF Program

- **Local Program Name:** Family Assistance (FA)
- **Federal Parent Program:** TANF
- **Administering Agency:** OTDA; Local Social Services Districts
- **Policy Manual:** 18 NYCRR Part 369 (Family Assistance); OTDA ADMs
- **Target Population:** Families with children where at least one parent is absent, incapacitated, unemployed, or working part-time
- **Income Limit:** ≤ ~100% FPL (varies by district); net income after deductions must fall below the Shelter Allowance and Basic Needs Standard
  - Family of 3 gross income limit (illustrative): ≤ ~$2,265/month
- **Maximum Monthly Benefit (2026 estimates):**
  - Family of 3 in New York City: ~$789/month
  - Family of 3 upstate NY: ~$789/month (statewide standard for FA; note NYC supplement exists)
- **New York Time Limits:** 60 months cumulative (mirrors federal TANF limit)
- **Work Requirements:** Must participate in assigned work activities through the New York State Office of Workforce Development; Employment Preparation Services (EPS)

### New York — Safety Net Assistance (SNA) — State-Only Program

- **Local Program Name:** Safety Net Assistance (SNA)
- **Federal Parent Program:** State-funded (no direct federal TANF link)
- **Administering Agency:** OTDA; Local Social Services Districts
- **Policy Manual:** 18 NYCRR Part 370 (Safety Net Assistance)
- **Target Population:** Individuals and families who are NOT eligible for Family Assistance (FA): single adults, childless couples, families who have exhausted FA time limits, non-citizens ineligible for federal TANF
- **Income Limit:** Similar to FA — net income below state needs standard
- **Maximum Monthly Benefit:** Cash SNA: Family of 3 ~$400/month; Non-cash SNA (voucher only): may be issued as vendor payments directly to landlords/utilities after 24 months of SNA receipt
- **SNA Time Limits:** 24 months cash assistance; after 24 months converts to non-cash (vendor payments) unless an exemption applies
- **Note:** New York's SNA program is a key safety net for populations excluded from the federal TANF system

### New York — Medicaid (Expansion State)

- **Local Program Name:** New York Medicaid
- **Federal Parent Program:** Medicaid (Title XIX)
- **Administering Agency:** NYSDOH, Office of Health Insurance Programs (OHIP)
- **Policy Manual:** Medicaid Reference Guide (MRG) — https://www.health.ny.gov/health_care/medicaid/reference
- **Expansion Status:** New York fully adopted ACA Medicaid expansion; New York has historically had one of the most expansive Medicaid programs in the nation
- **2026 Income Limits:**
  - Adults 19–64 (expansion): ≤ 138% FPL (MAGI-based)
    - Individual: ≤ $1,836/month
    - Family of 4: ≤ $3,795/month
  - Children under 19: ≤ 400% FPL covered through Medicaid and Child Health Plus (NY CHIP)
    - Child in family of 4 at 400%: ≤ $11,000/month
  - Pregnant individuals: ≤ 223% FPL
    - Individual: ≤ $2,966/month
  - Seniors 65+/Disabled (Non-MAGI): Income ≤ $934/month (individual); asset ≤ $16,800 (individual) — verify annually with NYSDOH as these adjust
- **New York Essential Plan:** For adults 138%–200% FPL who are not Medicaid eligible — subsidized health plan with very low or $0 premium
  - Individual at 150% FPL: ≤ $1,995/month → Essential Plan with $0 premium
- **Delivery Model:** New York Medicaid Managed Care; some fee-for-service remains for certain populations
- **Application Portal:** NY State of Health Marketplace — https://nystateofhealth.ny.gov (covers Medicaid, Essential Plan, and Qualified Health Plans)

### New York — Child Health Plus (CH+) — State CHIP Program

- **Local Program Name:** Child Health Plus (Child Health Plus, or "CH+")
- **Federal Parent Program:** CHIP (Title XXI)
- **Administering Agency:** NYSDOH
- **Policy Manual:** Child Health Plus Regulations — 10 NYCRR Part 504
- **Income Limit:**
  - Children under 19: ≤ 400% FPL (above Medicaid threshold of 138%–223% FPL depending on age)
  - Family of 4 at 400% FPL: ≤ $11,000/month
- **Premium:**
  - $0 premium for families ≤ 160% FPL
  - Sliding scale up to $60/month per child for families 160%–400% FPL
- **Benefit:** Comprehensive pediatric health coverage including dental, vision, behavioral health
- **Application:** NY State of Health — https://nystateofhealth.ny.gov

### New York — Child Care Subsidy (CCAP / CCDF)

- **Local Program Name:** New York Child Care Assistance Program (CCAP) / Low-Income Child Care Subsidy
- **Federal Parent Program:** CCDF
- **Administering Agency:** OTDA and Office of Children and Family Services (OCFS); administered locally by Social Services Districts
- **Policy Manual:** 18 NYCRR Part 415 (Child Day Care Centers); OTDA ADM 17-ADM-04 and subsequent updates on CCAP
- **Income Limit:** ≤ 85% New York State Median Income (SMI)
  - 2026 estimate: Family of 4 ≤ ~$7,800–$8,400/month (85% SMI; varies by region — NYC SMI is higher than upstate)
- **Copayment (Family Share):** Sliding scale from $0 to $160/biweekly based on income and family size; families at or below poverty level pay $0
- **2022 Reform Note:** OCFS significantly expanded CCAP in 2022–2024 to cover more families and eliminate waitlists in many regions
- **NYC-Specific:** NYC Administration for Children's Services (ACS) coordinates childcare vouchers and EarlyLearn NYC for eligible families in the 5 boroughs

### New York — Additional State-Specific Programs

- **Earned Income Credit (NYS EIC):** State-level refundable credit equal to 30% of the federal EITC; additional NYC EIC of 10% of federal EITC for NYC residents
- **Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program Education (SNAP-Ed):** NY Nutrition Network provides nutrition education at the community level
- **HEAP (Home Energy Assistance Program):** New York's LIHEAP implementation — helps with heating and cooling bills
  - Income Limit: ≤ 60% NYS Median Income (~$3,400/month for family of 4)
- **NYS Universal Pre-K (UPK):** Funded through the state Education Department; children turning 4 by December 1 of the school year are eligible regardless of income; administered by school districts
- **ACCESS HRA (NYC Only):** NYC Human Resources Administration's portal for SNAP, Cash Assistance, Medicaid, and other NYC-specific benefits — https://www.access.nyc.gov
- **Homebase (NYC Only):** Homelessness prevention program for NYC households at risk of eviction
